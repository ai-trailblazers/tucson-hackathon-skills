# positioning-statement

Hackathon fork of the BB positioning workflow. Writes to `positioning.md` in the team's repo.

## The 5 boxes (fill in this order)

1. **Competitive alternatives** — what your customer would use if you didn't exist? (List 2-3. "Nothing" is a real answer.)
2. **Unique attributes** — what do you have that those don't? (Concrete features, data, expertise — not adjectives.)
3. **Value (and proof)** — what does each attribute give the customer? Connect attribute → benefit → evidence.
4. **Target market characteristics** — who is the customer that values this MOST? (Specific traits, not demographics.)
5. **Market category** — what shelf are you on, in customer's head? ("Like X, but for Y" is fine.)

```markdown
## One-paragraph statement

For [target market] who [problem statement], [product name] is a [market category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [primary alternative], we [unique differentiator that's hard to copy].

## What this lets us cut
List things you were tempted to build/say that don't fit this positioning. These go in the kill pile.
```

## Rules

- Concrete > clever. "For solo bookkeepers running QuickBooks" beats "for modern professionals."
- The category is YOUR call — pick the one that makes the value obvious. Wrong category = wrong reference price.
- If 3+ "unique attributes" actually exist in competitor products, they're not unique. Cut them.
- Revisit after every 5 customer conversations. Positioning shifts as you learn.
